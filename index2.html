Nigeria's Economy Shows Signs of Growth, Says IMF

Published: March 3, 2026

The International Monetary Fund (IMF) has expressed optimism about Nigeria's economic prospects, citing improvements in oil production and agricultural output. According to the IMF, the country's GDP growth is expected to reach 3.2% in 2026.

The Nigerian government has implemented various reforms aimed at boosting economic growth, including measures to improve the business environment and increase foreign investment. Tech Startup Ecosystem Thrives in Lagos, Nigeria

Published: February 25, 2026

Lagos, Nigeria's commercial capital, is fast becoming a hub for tech startups in Africa. With a growing number of incubators, accelerators, and coworking spaces, entrepreneurs are finding it easier to launch and scale their businesses.

The city has seen a surge in investment from local and international investors, with notable startups securing millions of dollars in funding. As a result, Lagos is now considered one of the top tech hubs in Africa, attracting talent and innovation from across the continent.Read more.

Nigerian Authors Make Waves in International Literary Scene

Published: February 20, 2026

Nigerian authors are gaining recognition globally, with several books winning prestigious awards and topping bestseller lists. Writers like Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie and Wole Soyinka continue to inspire a new generation of authors.
